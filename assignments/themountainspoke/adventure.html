Biking Adventures and Fun!

For a change of pace and a fun way to get to know the area, try exploring Bozeman, Montana and the surrounding area on a bike.
The Mountain Spoke offers adventures for all, from skilled rider to family groups. Come and enjoy the beauty and nature.

Emerald Lake/Heather Lake

  • Trail Location - South of Bozeman down Hyalite Canyon Road, about a mile past the waterfall.
  • Trail Type - 100% single track mountain bike trail.
  • Distance - Out and back 9 miles
  • Skill Level - Advanced skills
  • Cost - $35 per person or $150 per group

West Bridger/Foothills Road

  • Trail Location - Found on the North edge of Bozeman along the Western flank of the Bridger Ranage.
  • Trail Type - mountain bike
  • Distance - 13.7 total miles
  • Skill Level - Easy to Moderate
  • Cost - $25 per person or $100 per group.

Sourdough Ridge Loop

  • Trail Location - Located on the east edge of Bozeman's city limits.
  • Trail Type - 1.6 miles on paved road; 1.9 miles on single track or gravel path.
  • Distance – 3.5 total miles
  • Skill Level - Mostly easy, with two short but strenuous climbs.
  • Cost - $20 per person or $90 per group

Gallagator Trail

  • Trail Location - Located in Bozeman, running southwest from South Church to the Museum of The Rockies on Kagy Boulevard.
  • Trail Type - An easy, off-road family ride in town; duck ponds; Museum of the Rockies.
  • Distance - 2.2 total miles
  • Skill Level – Easy
  • Cost - $15 per person or $50 per group



The Mountain Spoke Bike Park

The Mountain Spoke has a new attraction this year, The Spoke Park. All are invited to use the ramps and rails, steps and sidewalks. It has designated areas for each skill levels. Each area is surrounded by a chain link fence so that people can see all the action going on. For the safety of those using the park, helmets must be worn at all times. There will also be safety personnel who will help to keep things safe and to guide others to the correct skill area. It only costs $2 a day. Enjoy!
